Research Experience

My research experience spans natural language processing, reliable machine learning, software security, speech and audio analysis, sensing systems, and biomedical AI. The common thread is reliability. I study how models behave when the data is complex, noisy, shifted, or tied to real-world decision making.

At Auburn University, my current work focuses on reliable and context-aware NLP. This includes sentiment analysis under linguistic complexity, distribution shift, aspect-level opinion, software vulnerability analysis, and speech model diagnostics. Earlier work at Universiti Malaysia Pahang focused on applied machine learning for biomedical sensing and ultra-wideband systems.

This page summarizes my research experience by institution and role. Detailed descriptions of selected systems and manuscripts are provided on the Notable Projects page.

Institution-Based Research Experience

Auburn University
Ph.D. Research, Graduate Research Assistant, and Collaborative Research | 2022–Present

My Ph.D. research at Auburn University focuses on reliable and context-aware machine learning. The main dissertation direction studies sentiment analysis when text contains linguistic complexity. I also work on related problems in software security, speech representation analysis, traffic data imputation, and sensing systems.

This research combines transformer-based modeling, uncertainty estimation, graph learning, hierarchical evidence modeling, and task-aware evaluation. The goal is to build methods that remain useful beyond clean benchmark settings.

ShiftAware-Select Reliable NLP classification under distribution shift. Target venue: IEEE ICMLA.
CAGLSAv2 Discourse-span attention for length-robust sentiment analysis. Target venue: IEEE ICMLA.
Irony-Aware Twitter Sentiment Analysis Multitask learning for sentiment, irony, and emotion. Submission in progress: IEEE AIBThings.
Hierarchical Severity Classification Vulnerability severity prediction with automatic CWE enrichment. Submission in progress: IEEE AIBThings.
DualGraph-RoBERTa Aspect-based sentiment analysis using syntactic and semantic graph evidence. Submission in progress: IEEE AIBThings.
Discourse-Aware Domain Adaptation Sentiment transfer across domains using discourse and span-level evidence. In progress.
LayerLens Task-relevant layer disruption analysis for self-supervised speech encoders. Target venue: IEEE/ACM TASLP.
Vi-Detect Smartphone-based vibration sensing for component looseness detection. ACM SenSys poster and ACM TOSN journal work.
DeepFakeDetect Deepfake audio detection using mechanical modeling of vocal folds. Journal direction: JISA / security venues.
Traffic Data Imputation Study Downstream evaluation of reconstruction accuracy and forecasting utility. Journal direction: IEEE T-ITS.
