Research Experience

My research focuses on reliable and context-aware machine learning for natural language processing, large language models, AI for cybersecurity, sensing systems, and biomedical AI. I study how models behave when data are complex, noisy, shifted, incomplete, or tied to real-world decision making.

At Auburn University, my Ph.D. research focuses on reliable and context-aware NLP. This work includes sentiment analysis under linguistic complexity, distribution shift, irony understanding, aspect-level opinion, cybersecurity text analytics, software vulnerability analysis, and speech model diagnostics. Earlier work at Universiti Malaysia Pahang focused on applied machine learning for biomedical sensing and ultra-wideband systems.

Detailed descriptions of selected systems and manuscripts are provided on the Notable Projects page.

Research Experience by Academic Institution

Auburn University
Graduate Research Assistant | Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering | 2022–Present

My Ph.D. research at Auburn University develops reliable and context-aware AI methods for language understanding, cybersecurity decision support, speech representation analysis, intelligent transportation, and sensing systems. The main dissertation direction studies sentiment analysis when text contains linguistic complexity such as contrast, irony, implicit meaning, aspect-specific opinion, and distribution shift.

This research combines transformer-based modeling, uncertainty estimation, graph learning, multi-task learning, hierarchical evidence modeling, and task-aware evaluation. My goal is to build methods that remain useful beyond clean benchmark settings and can support practical decisions in real-world computing environments.

ShiftAware-Select Reliable NLP classification under distribution shift with calibration, uncertainty estimation, and abstain-to-human decisioning. Under review: IEEE ICMLA 2026.
CAGLSAv2 Position-invariant discourse-span attention for contrastive and length-robust sentiment analysis. Under review: IEEE ICMLA 2026.
Irony-Aware Twitter Sentiment Analysis Multi-task learning framework for sentiment, irony, and emotion understanding in social media text. Submitted: IEEE AIBThings 2026.
Hierarchical Severity Classification Cybersecurity vulnerability severity prediction with automatic CWE enrichment and sentence-level evidence. Submitted: IEEE AIBThings 2026.
DualGraph-RoBERTa Aspect-based sentiment analysis using syntactic dependency graphs and semantic co-occurrence evidence. Submitted: IEEE AIBThings 2026.
LayerLens Task-relevant layer disruption analysis for self-supervised speech encoders under noise, reverberation, and enhancement artifacts. Under review: IEEE/ACM TASLP.
Traffic Data Imputation Study Downstream evaluation of reconstruction accuracy and forecasting utility for intelligent transportation data. Under review: IEEE T-ITS.
Vi-Detect Smartphone-based vibration sensing for fine-grained component looseness detection. Poster published at ACM SenSys; full article under minor revision at ACM TOSN.
DeepFakeDetect Fine-grained deepfake audio detection using mechanical modeling of vocal folds. Under review: Journal of Information Security and Applications.
