Teaching

My teaching experience spans computer science, software engineering, project-based learning, grading, mentoring, and academic support. At Auburn University, I have served as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for multiple semesters while supporting large undergraduate and graduate courses.

My teaching work focuses on helping students move from technical concepts to working systems. I have supported student teams, reviewed project progress, graded technical assignments, held office hours, managed course logistics, and helped students communicate their design and implementation decisions more clearly.

Teaching Experience

Graduate Teaching Assistant: Senior Design Project
Undergraduate capstone course, Auburn University, 2022–Present

I have supported Senior Design Project for eight consecutive semesters at Auburn University. This course requires students to work in teams, interact with project sponsors, develop working software systems, and present their progress through design reviews, demonstrations, and final deliverables.

My role has involved mentoring student teams, supporting project planning, reviewing progress, grading technical submissions, helping with demonstrations, managing course logistics, and communicating expectations across large classes. This experience has strengthened my ability to guide students through open-ended software engineering problems where technical work, teamwork, documentation, and communication all matter.

  • Supported large classes with more than 100 students per semester.
  • Guided student teams through project planning, milestones, demonstrations, and final presentations.
  • Reviewed technical reports, design documents, implementation progress, and sponsor-facing deliverables.
  • Helped students improve teamwork, communication, and software engineering practice.
Graduate Teaching Assistant: Software Quality Assurance
Undergraduate and graduate course, Auburn University, multiple semesters

I have served as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for Software Quality Assurance for three semesters. The course covers software testing, quality processes, verification, validation, and practical techniques for improving software reliability.

My responsibilities included grading assignments and exams, holding office hours, answering student questions, supporting course logistics, and providing feedback on technical work. This role gave me direct experience with both undergraduate and graduate students and helped me develop a clear, student-centered style for explaining software engineering concepts.

  • Supported grading for assignments, exams, and course activities.
  • Held office hours and helped students understand testing and quality assurance concepts.
  • Provided feedback on technical work and helped students connect course ideas to practical software development.
  • Assisted with Canvas updates, course communication, and student support.
