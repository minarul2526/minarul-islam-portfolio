Welcome to my personal homepage

My name is Minarul Islam. I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering at Auburn University. My research focuses on reliable and context-aware machine learning for natural language, speech, software security, and AI systems.

My dissertation work focuses on Reliable and Context-Aware Sentiment Analysis under Linguistic Complexity. Many language models perform well on standard benchmarks, but they can still make confident mistakes when text contains contrast, irony, implicit meaning, aspect-specific opinion, or domain shift. My work studies how to make these models more reliable and easier to understand in such settings.

My research develops methods that use context, structure, uncertainty, and task-specific evidence to improve model behavior. The broader goal is to build AI systems that are accurate, reliable, interpretable, and useful in real-world applications.

Featured Research Projects

  1. ShiftAware-Select: Reliable NLP classification under distribution shift using uncertainty estimation, calibration, and abstain-to-human decisioning to reduce confident errors.
  2. CAGLSAv2: Context-aware sentiment analysis method that models contrastive clause information to better understand sentiment in linguistically complex text.
  3. Irony-Aware Multitask Sentiment Learning: Multitask learning framework that connects sentiment prediction with irony and emotion awareness for social media text.
  4. Hierarchical Severity Classification with CWE Enrichment: Software vulnerability severity classification using sentence-level evidence and structured security knowledge.
  5. DualGraph-RoBERTa for Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis: Graph-enhanced transformer model for aspect-level opinion understanding with structural and contextual evidence.
  6. Discourse-Aware Domain Adaptation for Sentiment Analysis: Ongoing work on improving sentiment model transfer across domains using discourse-aware and span-level evidence.
