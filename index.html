About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Software Engineering at Auburn University. My research focuses on reliable and context-aware machine learning methods for natural language, speech, and security applications. I am interested in developing AI systems that are robust, interpretable, and useful in real-world settings.

My work spans natural language processing, sentiment analysis, speech processing, software and AI security, and sensor-based systems. I also have extensive teaching experience as a Graduate Teaching Assistant at Auburn University.

Research Interests

Machine Learning Natural Language Processing Deep Learning Sentiment Analysis Speech Processing AI Security Software Engineering Sensor Systems

My current research investigates how AI models can make more reliable decisions under distribution shift, linguistic complexity, non-literal language, and noisy real-world inputs.
